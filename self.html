What is Self Psychology?


Self psychology indicates the importance of external relationships in protecting one's self-worth and integrity. According to this approach, inspired by Heinz Kohut's books, people need positive reactions from other people to feel good. That's why some observers also call this theory "two-person psychology". This approach was developed as a result of Kohut's observations in the psychoanalytic treatment of people with narcissistic disorders. Patients could change their self-worth according to the attitude of the people around them, and Kohut observed that these patients developed two types of transferas.

  1. Mirror Transference
  2. Idealizing Transference

Mirror Transference


In mirror transfer, the patient always seeks the approval and appreciation of the therapist.
According to me this can be likened to your friend showing you the new outfit he bought and expecting appreciation and praise.


Idealizing Transference

"Idealization transference" means that in this process, the patient sees the therapist as an overly positive or perfect person. This manifests itself as the patient's excessive admiration for the therapist or perceiving him or her as a perfect figure. This type of transference can cause the patient to develop unrealistic expectations and create dynamics that can affect the therapeutic process. By recognizing and working with this situation, therapists help the patient develop realistic expectations.

Self Object Term

It was used by Kohut to describe the role of the person to whom mirroring and idealization needs are directed and the people who meet these needs. These people, called self-objects, can be described as functions rather than humans.
Kohut mentioned that the need for self-objects will last a lifetime.
