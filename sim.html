Simulation Theory

26/08/24


Simulation Universe, What Awaits Us in the Future?


In recent years, the advances made in simulation have become dizzying. Such great progress has been made that when we look at simulations of the Universe, we sometimes have difficulty distinguishing them from real images. And this is just the beginning. Let's take Elon Musk's example in this regard: While 40 years ago we were playing Tetris, now we are intertwined with hyper-realistic computer games. What will happen 40 years from now? What will be achieved in the fields of simulation and augmented reality? The simulations developed will probably be so realistic that we will be able to define it as ‘even more realistic than reality.’ Simulations that will make our brain believe that it is completely in a reality will enter our lives.



Marie Lu's Wildcard book series, which I read, contains very creative examples of this. The book discusses how real life strategies are integrated and simulated in games. Customisable lenses worn on people's eyes, whose presence is not felt, help them in all their lives, they follow the yellow strip that appears on the ground while navigating, and as soon as they open the game, they completely isolate themselves from the real world. They found themselves in a simulation and their brains were controlled. After reading this book, I thought, ‘I wonder if there are structures that control our brains or try to direct our interests in a technological device that is popular in the world.’ Surely a similar technological device is used. For example, sites and products appear according to our interests. But what I am talking about is the development of systems that can manage our brain. Things that can be done to us against our will.



If we're in a simulation, our ID number reminds me of our player ID. It feels like a sequence of numbers unique to each player. As most people would think, ‘Could the imperfections in the universe be a by-product of the simulation?’ I think non-human natural disasters like earthquakes and volcanic eruptions could be the fault of the simulation.
In the film The Matrix, we know that the deja vu experience is attributed to simulation (system) failure. Could this really be true?
The fact that when we enter the sea, the end of the sea is like a line, but as we swim, new sea water is added, just like in minecraft, makes me think of the existence of simulation.
